The Guardians of Time

For millennia, clocks have been the guardians of our understanding of time.

From the shadow cast on a sundial to the precision of atomic watches, these instruments have evolved to measure time with ever-greater accuracy.

Each era brought its own innovation, marking crucial milestones in the evolution of our relationship with time.



Key Points to Explore

From Sundials to Atomic Clocks

The evolution of clocks, from simple sundials to atomic clocks capable of measuring infinitesimally small fractions of time, traces a fascinating path of human progress.

Each of these innovations reflects humanity's ambition to understand and tame time.

The Symbolism of Clocks in Culture

Clocks are often used as symbols of the inexorable passage of time, mortality, but also discipline and regularity.

This symbolism has evolved over time, and it continues to fascinate artists, writers, and philosophers.

Clocks in Possible Futures

In a future where we might travel through time or manipulate it, clocks could take unexpected forms.

They could become tools for navigating through time, beyond their simple function of measurement.


Why the Clock for the Prehistory of the Future?


Clocks are the central symbol of our quest to capture, measure, and understand time. They represent both the past, where humanity sought to master the regularity of days and nights, and the future, with technological innovations bringing us closer to the exact measurement of time. In the context of the Prehistory of the Future, clocks serve as a bridge between humanity's earliest attempts to measure time and future conceptions where time could be modified, stretched, or even controlled.
